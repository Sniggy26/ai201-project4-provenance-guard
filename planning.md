## Architecture

### Submission Flow
POST /submit

→ validate input (text, creator_id)

→ Signal 1: LLM classification (Groq)

→ Signal 2: Stylometric heuristics

→ Confidence scoring (weighted average)

→ Transparency label generation

→ Write to audit log

→ Return response (content_id, attribution, confidence, label)

### Appeal Flow
POST /appeal

→ validate input (content_id, creator_reasoning)

→ look up original classification in log

→ update status to "under_review"

→ write appeal entry to audit log

→ return confirmation

### Endpoints
- `POST /submit` — accepts text + creator_id, returns classification
- `POST /appeal` — accepts content_id + creator_reasoning, updates status
- `GET /log` — returns recent audit log entries

## Detection Signals

### Signal 1: LLM Classification (Groq)
- **What it measures:** Semantic and stylistic coherence holistically — whether the writing reads as AI-generated based on vocabulary patterns, sentence uniformity, and phrasing conventions
- **Output:** A score between 0.0 (likely human) and 1.0 (likely AI)
- **Why:** A large language model can assess writing holistically in ways that rule-based systems can't — it picks up on unnatural fluency, overuse of transition phrases, and lack of personal voice
- **Blind spot:** Will struggle with highly polished human writing (academic papers, professional blogs) that shares surface features with AI output. Also unreliable on very short texts.

### Signal 2: Stylometric Heuristics
- **What it measures:** Statistical properties of the text — sentence length variance, type-token ratio (vocabulary diversity), and punctuation density
- **Output:** A score between 0.0 (likely human) and 1.0 (likely AI)
- **Why:** AI writing tends to be statistically uniform — consistent sentence lengths, moderate vocabulary diversity, predictable punctuation. Human writing is messier and more variable.
- **Blind spot:** Non-native English speakers and formal/academic human writers may produce text that scores high on AI likelihood purely due to writing style, not because it is AI-generated.

### Combining Signals
Weighted average: `confidence = (0.6 × llm_score) + (0.4 × stylo_score)`

LLM signal weighted higher because it captures semantic patterns that heuristics miss. Stylometric signal acts as a corrective — if LLM scores high but stylometrics score low (high variance, rich vocabulary), confidence is pulled down toward uncertain.

## Uncertainty Representation

| Score Range | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| 0.00 – 0.35 | Likely human |
| 0.36 – 0.64 | Uncertain |
| 0.65 – 1.00 | Likely AI |

A score of 0.6 means: both signals lean slightly toward AI but neither is confident. The label should communicate uncertainty, not a verdict. A score of 0.85 means both signals are in strong agreement that the text is AI-generated.

False positive asymmetry: mislabeling a human writer's work as AI is worse than missing AI-generated content. This is reflected in the thresholds — the "likely AI" band doesn't start until 0.65, giving human writers more room before a confident verdict is returned.

## Transparency Label Variants

### High-confidence AI (score ≥ 0.65)
⚠️ AI-Generated Content

Our system found strong indicators that this content was AI-generated.

Confidence: [XX]%

This label is based on automated analysis and may not be perfect.

The creator can submit an appeal if this is incorrect.

### Uncertain (score 0.36 – 0.64)
🔍 Attribution Uncertain

Our system could not confidently determine whether this content was written by a human or generated by AI.

Confidence: [XX]%

Treat this content with that uncertainty in mind.

The creator can submit an appeal to provide additional context.

### High-confidence human (score ≤ 0.35)
✅ Likely Human-Written

Our system found strong indicators that this content was written by a human.

Confidence: [XX]%

This label is based on automated analysis and may not be perfect.

## Appeals Workflow

- **Who can appeal:** Any creator identified by their creator_id
- **What they provide:** content_id (from the original /submit response) and creator_reasoning (free text explanation)
- **What the system does:**
    1. Looks up the original classification by content_id
    2. Updates status from "classified" to "under_review"
    3. Logs the appeal with the original decision, the new status, and the creator's reasoning
    4. Returns a confirmation message to the creator
- **What a human reviewer sees:** The full audit log entry — original confidence score, both signal scores, attribution result, and the creator's written reasoning

## Anticipated Edge Cases

**1. Non-native English speakers:** A human writer with formal, grammatically uniform writing may score high on stylometric heuristics (low sentence variance, moderate TTR) while also being flagged by the LLM signal. The system may confidently misclassify them as AI. The appeals workflow exists specifically for this case — the label text explicitly names this as a possibility.

**2. Lightly edited AI output:** A user who generates text with AI and then edits it substantially may produce content that scores in the uncertain range. Neither signal can reliably detect AI text that has been meaningfully revised. The system should return "uncertain" rather than a confident verdict in this case.

**3. Very short texts:** A haiku or a two-sentence post gives both signals very little to work with. Sentence length variance is meaningless at 2 sentences. The LLM signal is unreliable on short text. The system should flag short submissions as uncertain regardless of signal output.
